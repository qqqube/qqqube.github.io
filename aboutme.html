Hello World!



I'm Lauren, a coffee enthusiast from Huntington Beach, California currently pursuing degrees in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics from UC Berkeley. As an aspiring software engineer, I am particularly interested in Machine Learning, Data Science, and Computer Graphics.


Having entered Berkeley without any programming experience, I definitely did not expect to pick up Computer Science as a major. During my freshman year, I decided to enroll in an introductory Data Science course that covered basic programming fundamentals and immediately became drawn to the world of technology, as well as Berkeley's unique structure of Computer Science education. Succeeding in my quest of declaring the major would not have been possible without the help and support of the instructors that I met along the way. My experience during my first few semesters encouraged me to become an instructor as well, in hopes of being able to inspire prospective software engineers as much as my former instructors had inspired me. During my undergraduate career, I served as a TA for one of the core Computer Sciences courses in the EECS curriculum, "Data Structures and Programming Methodology", for four semesters. Not only was this experience rewarding from the various interactions I had with my students, but it was also a great learning opportunity for me in that I was able to strengthen my debugging/development skills and additionally gain a deeper understanding of the conceptual details covered in the course. I believe my teaching experience will play an integral part in my future as an engineer, and will always be grateful for having been a part of Berkeley's instructional community.


Outside of academics, I enjoy introspecting, going for long runs, binge-watching Korean dramas, and jamming out on Spotify [disclaimer: I don't do all these things simultaneously]. I'm constantly seeking opportunities to improve myself and especially enjoy problem-solving and tackling new challenges, both technical and non-technical.
